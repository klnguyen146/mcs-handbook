# BOOKS.md

> Canonical Computer Science Reading List for the Master Computer Science Handbook (MCSH)

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official collection of textbooks and reference books recommended throughout the Master Computer Science Handbook.

Rather than attempting to list every available Computer Science book, this document identifies the most influential, academically respected, and pedagogically valuable references.

Every recommendation in the handbook should primarily originate from this document.

---

# Design Philosophy

Books are selected based on the following principles:

- Academic authority
- Long-term influence
- Graduate-level quality
- Clear explanations
- Strong engineering relevance
- Wide adoption by universities

Preference should be given to books that remain valuable for many years rather than temporary publications.

# Usage Guidelines

Volumes should recommend:

- One primary textbook.
- One supplementary textbook.
- Selected landmark papers.
- Official documentation (if applicable).

Avoid recommending excessive reading.

Quality is preferred over quantity.

# Maintenance Policy

New books should only be added if they:

- Achieve broad academic recognition.
- Become widely adopted in graduate education.
- Offer significant educational value.

Books with short-term popularity should generally be excluded.

---

# Final Goal

This document defines the canonical reading list for the Master Computer Science Handbook.

Its purpose is to ensure that every Volume recommends authoritative, high-quality references, enabling readers to build a strong academic foundation while avoiding fragmented or inconsistent learning resources.
