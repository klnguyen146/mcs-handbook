# TIMELINE.md

> Historical Timeline of Computer Science for the Master Computer Science Handbook (MCSH)

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official historical timeline referenced throughout the Master Computer Science Handbook.

Rather than serving as a complete history of Computer Science, it highlights the major milestones that fundamentally changed the evolution of the field.

Every Volume should reference this timeline when introducing historical context.

---

# Design Principles

The timeline should:

- Focus on transformational events.
- Emphasize causal relationships.
- Highlight paradigm shifts.
- Cover both theoretical and engineering milestones.
- Remain concise and academically accurate.

Historical anecdotes that do not significantly influence Computer Science should be omitted.

---

- Limits of formal systems

---

Contribution:

# Usage Guidelines

Volumes should not reproduce the entire timeline.

Instead:

- Reference relevant milestones.
- Explain why a milestone changed the field.
- Connect historical events to modern technologies.

History should support conceptual understanding rather than act as trivia.

---

# Maintenance Policy

This timeline should only be updated when an event demonstrates long-term significance for Computer Science.

Temporary trends, short-lived technologies, or commercial announcements should not be included.

---

# Final Goal

The timeline provides a shared historical framework for the Master Computer Science Handbook.

It enables every Volume to place concepts within their historical context, helping readers understand not only **what** Computer Science is, but also **how** it evolved into its current form and **why** each major breakthrough mattered.
