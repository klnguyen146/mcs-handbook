# Purpose

This volume serves as the central technical volume of the Master Computer Science Handbook.

It unifies the theoretical foundations and engineering practices of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Data Mining, Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, and Generative AI into a coherent learning journey.

Rather than treating these as independent university courses, this volume demonstrates how they are interconnected within modern intelligent systems.

Readers should develop the ability to understand, design, implement, evaluate, and deploy AI-driven solutions while appreciating the mathematical principles, algorithmic foundations, and system considerations behind every model.

# Core Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence is not a single algorithm.

It is a complete ecosystem that combines:

- Mathematics
- Statistics
- Algorithms
- Data
- Knowledge
- Learning
- Reasoning
- Optimization
- Computing Systems

Every technique should answer:

- What problem does it solve?
- Why was it invented?
- What mathematical principles support it?
- What assumptions does it make?
- When should it be used?
- What are its limitations?
- How does it compare with alternative approaches?

Readers should understand the evolution of AI as a scientific discipline rather than memorize isolated models.
