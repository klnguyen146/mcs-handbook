# Purpose

This volume serves as the capstone of the Master Computer Science Handbook.

It guides readers through the complete journey of conducting a Master's thesis, from selecting a research topic to publishing scientific papers and defending the thesis.

Rather than teaching isolated academic skills, this volume integrates knowledge from all previous volumes into a coherent research workflow.

Readers will learn how to transform technical expertise into original scientific contributions.

---

# Target Audience

This volume is intended for readers who:

- Have completed Volumes 01–07.
- Are beginning their Master's thesis.
- Plan to publish conference or journal papers.
- Aspire to continue toward PhD research.
- Wish to become independent Computer Science researchers.

---

# Position in the Handbook

This is the final volume of the Master Computer Science Handbook.

It integrates mathematics, computer science, algorithms, systems, artificial intelligence, and research methodology into one complete research process.

This volume represents the transition from student to researcher.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this volume, readers should be able to:

- Select a meaningful research topic.
- Conduct a comprehensive literature review.
- Identify research gaps.
- Formulate research questions and hypotheses.
- Design and implement research methodologies.
- Build reproducible experimental systems.
- Analyze and interpret experimental results.
- Write a complete Master's thesis.
- Prepare conference and journal publications.
- Successfully defend a thesis.
- Plan future research directions.

---

# Core Philosophy

A thesis is not simply a graduation requirement.

A thesis is evidence that the researcher can independently create new scientific knowledge.

Every thesis should answer:

- What problem is being solved?
- Why is the problem important?
- What is the novelty?
- How is the solution evaluated?
- Why should the community trust the results?
- What is the scientific contribution?
- What future work remains?

Readers should focus on contribution rather than completion.
