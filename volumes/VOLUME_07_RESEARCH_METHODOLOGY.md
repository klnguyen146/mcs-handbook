# Purpose

This volume develops the mindset, methodology, and practical skills required to conduct scientific research in Computer Science.

Rather than focusing solely on academic writing, this volume teaches readers how to formulate research questions, review literature, design experiments, evaluate results, communicate findings, and contribute new knowledge to the scientific community.

It bridges the transition from engineering practice to evidence-based scientific inquiry.

---

# Target Audience

This volume is intended for readers who:

- Have completed the technical foundations in Volumes 01–06.
- Plan to pursue a Master's thesis or PhD.
- Want to publish scientific papers.
- Need to conduct rigorous experimental research.
- Wish to understand how scientific knowledge is created and validated.

---

# Position in the Handbook

This is the seventh volume of the Master Computer Science Handbook.

It transforms readers from engineers who build systems into researchers who investigate, evaluate, and create new knowledge.

This volume directly prepares readers for Volume 08 — Master's Thesis.

# Learning Objectives

After completing this volume, readers should be able to:

- Understand the scientific research process.
- Formulate meaningful research questions.
- Conduct systematic literature reviews.
- Critically evaluate existing work.
- Design reproducible experiments.
- Apply statistical methods to analyze results.
- Write scientific papers using international standards.
- Review and respond to peer feedback.
- Practice ethical and responsible research.

---

# Core Philosophy

Research is not the act of writing a paper.

Research is the process of discovering reliable knowledge.

Every research project should answer:

- What problem exists?
- Why is it important?
- What has already been done?
- What gap remains?
- What is the proposed solution?
- Why should others believe the results?
- How can the work be reproduced?
- What new knowledge has been contributed?

Readers should learn to think like scientists rather than simply completing academic requirements.
